This site consists of online portals to the research projects and materials supporting the research of Timothy L. Hinrichs.

  • Examples: examples of different reformulation techniques that result in improved efficiency.
  • References: literature that in some way or another addresses representation and reasoning about knowledge expressed in a logical form.
  • CL: AMA: resources for our textbook Computational Logic: A Modern Approach.
  • Jack: various reasoning and reformulation tools for declarative languages.
  • Plato: a tool for automatically generating web forms from declarative descriptions of those forms.
  • Verona: a basic system for exploring the tag-based authorization paradigm, using the database of references as data.
